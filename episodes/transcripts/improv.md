# YY & Me, Episode 8: Improv

This episode is going to be completely off the top of my head, without any drafting.

I want to see what comes out of my mouth when I have absolutely nothing planned.

For one thing, recording Episode Seven made me realize that my car is perfect for this.

The sound is dead. It has acoustic glass. Quite frankly, it’s better than my basement.

With the iPhone I have now, I think I’m going to record here from now on. It can be my little sanctuary away from all the crazy kids making noise all the time.

You can probably hear them in some of my YouTube videos. You can hear the kids walking around.

That gets me thinking: Is all that background noise annoying? Is it noticeable? Does anybody really care?

I could take the time to clean everything up and make it sound professional.

My brother and I made a CD a long time ago. We went through the process of learning sound engineering. We did the noise reduction, compression, normalization, trimming—all of it.

We made a nice CD, and I’m still proud of it.

But it took a long time.

Listen, when I was younger, I had a lot of time. Let’s not kid anybody.

If you’re in college, you’re lucky.

You think you’re busy now?

I don’t need to belabor the point.

Now I realize that I don’t have that much time.

I can’t spend twelve hours trying to get every episode exactly right. I don’t have twelve hours to rehearse every script.

So how am I supposed to make the difference I want to make in the world—or even just in the lives of my kids—if I don’t have time to clean everything up enough for them to listen?

Maybe it doesn’t matter.

Maybe they’ll listen anyway.

Or maybe they won’t.

Maybe they don’t care.

I honestly don’t know.

Even though I’ve said repeatedly that I’m making this for my kids, and also for builders and other people, I think the first consumer—and possibly the only consumer—is me.

You want to know what else is funny?

A lot of people don’t like the sound of their own voice. I don’t particularly like mine either.

But I’m noticing that the more I listen to myself talk, the easier it becomes for me to think.

Is that weird?

Maybe it’s a little weird.

AI doesn’t talk the way we talk.

In fact, if AI continues in the direction it’s going, I think all AI is eventually going to sound the same.

I was listening to a host on the *Everyday AI* podcast—I think that’s what it’s called. He talks a lot about AI and uses vocabulary that might sound unusual unless you also use AI.

To me, his vocabulary sounded completely normal.

Which probably means I’m on that spectrum too.

And that’s probably because I use AI so much.

So *YY & Me* is becoming my opportunity to learn how not to use that language.

It’s teaching me not to depend on AI to write everything.

Because I have become dependent on it.

I use it for work all the time. I even use it sometimes to write emails.

I know I probably shouldn’t admit that, but it’s true.

If you notice that an email ends with “Best,” followed by a comma, it was probably written by AI—unless the person sincerely says “Best.”

Although AI wouldn’t say it unless a lot of people already did.

At least, I think that’s how it works.

So where is this episode going?

I guess *YY & Me* needs to stay human.

I considered a few other options.

I thought about bringing in my synthetic voice clone. I thought about producing more episodes that were less polished, just to get my ideas out there.

In fact, I did clone my voice. I also made a collection of episodes drafted with AI.

But that might be another story for another day.

In the meantime, I guess improv has taught me that a lot can come out of my mouth when I don’t practice.

It may not be as consistent, clean, or censored as something produced with AI.

But every so often, I think it’s okay to unload.

That’s how we talk to one another.

It’s how we talk to our kids and our friends.

It would be incredibly strange if we had to look at our phones every time we wanted to talk to somebody.

So this is me continuing to teach myself how to speak without relying on AI.
